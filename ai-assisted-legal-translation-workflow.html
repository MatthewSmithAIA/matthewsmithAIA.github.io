AI-Assisted Legal Translation Workflow

Privacy-first workflow design for sensitive legal documents using OCR, PII masking, human review, translation preparation, and document recreation support.

AI Automation Externship · Early-Stage Prototype

Project Overview

ABC Consultants provides legal translation support for documents that require accuracy, confidentiality, and human review. During my AI automation externship, I independently designed and prototyped an early-stage AI-assisted workflow to support document intake, OCR, personally identifiable information (PII) protection, translation preparation, and document recreation.

The project was designed as a human-reviewed support system, not as a replacement for qualified legal translators or final legal review.

Business Problem

Legal translation workflows often involve scanned PDFs, inconsistent document formats, confidential personal information, terminology that must remain consistent, and final documents that need to preserve the appearance of the original. Performing every step manually can make the process slower, harder to track, and more vulnerable to privacy or quality-control errors.

My Role

I worked independently as the workflow designer and prototype builder. My responsibilities included mapping the process, creating intake and tracking systems, developing the OCR-enabled redaction application, designing placeholder-based PII protection, preparing AI review prompts, planning human-review gates, and documenting future automation options.

Proposed Workflow

  1. Receive job and document information through a structured intake form.
  2. Record job status, document status, privacy-review progress, and deliverables in Google Sheets trackers.
  3. Process uploaded PDFs through OCR to detect readable text and its coordinates, including text in scanned or image-based documents.
  4. Identify sensitive information and replace it with controlled placeholders before AI-assisted review or translation preparation.
  5. Generate privacy-safe PDF outputs for human review and later document recreation.
  6. Evaluate PII coverage using AI review prompts and flag potential omissions for human inspection.
  7. Approve the protected document and its placeholder map before the translation stage.
  8. Prepare the document for translated text overlay and controlled PII restoration after review.

What I Built and Designed

Job Intake and Tracking

Built a Google Form and Google Sheets system for job intake, document tracking, PII-review status, redaction outputs, translation preparation, and final deliverables.

OCR-Enabled Redactor

Developed a Python and FastAPI application using PyMuPDF and Google Cloud Vision to detect text and coordinates in digital and scanned legal PDFs.

Placeholder-Based PII Masking

Designed masking logic that replaces sensitive values with placeholders such as [PERSON_001], [ADDRESS_001], and [EMAIL_001].

Controlled PII Mapping

Created a piiMap structure to retain placeholder-to-value relationships for controlled restoration only after the protected document passed review.

Two PDF Output Types

Produced a privacy-safe placeholder reference PDF for verification and a whiteout template that preserves layout elements for translated document recreation.

AI-Assisted Privacy Review

Designed review prompts to evaluate whether PII coverage appeared complete and to flag possible missed sensitive information for a human reviewer.

Human-Review Gate

Added a checkpoint so protected documents and PII maps could be inspected and approved before translation-related processing continued.

RAG-Ready Architecture

Planned the workflow so approved glossaries, document-type rules, prior translation examples, and client terminology could later be retrieved to support more consistent translations.

Privacy-by-Design Approach

  • Sensitive values were represented by placeholders before AI-assisted processing.
  • Placeholder mappings were separated from the protected document.
  • Human review was required before translation preparation continued.
  • PII restoration was planned for the final document stage rather than before translated text was overlaid.
  • The workflow retained separate outputs for privacy review and final layout recreation.

Glossary and RAG Preparation

I began designing a glossary-supported structure to reduce terminology drift and prepare the system for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Under a future implementation, the workflow could retrieve approved legal terminology, document-specific instructions, previous translation examples, and client rules before generating translation support.

This RAG capability was part of the planned architecture and was not represented as a completed production knowledge system.

Challenges Addressed

  • Extracting text from both digital and image-based legal documents.
  • Preserving text coordinates so sensitive content could be located within the PDF.
  • Protecting PII without losing the ability to restore approved values later.
  • Separating privacy-review documents from document-recreation templates.
  • Designing a workflow that supports AI while retaining meaningful human oversight.
  • Planning for translation consistency without prematurely presenting the prototype as a finished production system.

Project Value

  • Demonstrated how AI automation can support a sensitive, document-heavy professional workflow.
  • Created a clearer structure for intake, status tracking, privacy review, and deliverable management.
  • Established a prototype for protecting personal information before AI-assisted processing.
  • Connected OCR, PDF processing, workflow automation, AI review, and human approval within one system design.
  • Produced technical documentation that could guide additional automation through Make, Google Drive, and local redactor processing.

It also strengthened my ability to design systems around privacy, human review, and the practical needs of the people responsible for using them.
